# FRCA Intermediate Curriculum — Items NOT Assessed by the Written Examination

Items below do **not** carry the **E** assessment method marker; they are assessed by direct observation (A), case-based discussion (C), DOPS (D), multi-source feedback (M), simulation (S), logbook (L), or similar workplace-based methods only.

---

## Neuroanaesthesia

### Skills

- **NA_IS_01** *(A)* — Demonstrates a full and focused preoperative assessment, followed by optimization, of patients presenting with neurological disease
- **NA_IS_02** *(A)* — Demonstrates understanding of the problems of obtaining consent in patients who are not competent, including those with impaired consciousness and confusion
- **NA_IS_03** *(A,C)* — Demonstrates provision of safe perioperative anaesthetic care for a variety of neurosurgical procedures including elective and emergency intracranial surgery, shunt surgery, and cervical and lumbar spinal surgery
- **NA_IS_04** *(D,A)* — Demonstrates the ability, via physiological and pharmacological manipulation, to improve intra-cranial homeostasis in pathological states
- **NA_IS_05** *(A,C)* — Demonstrates the ability to manage patients with acute head injuries for anaesthesia for emergency neurosurgery, non-surgical management if indicated, and on-going neuro critical care
- **NA_IS_06** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates safe patient positioning — prone, lateral (park bench)
- **NA_IS_07** *(A,C,D)* — Demonstrates the ability to resuscitate, stabilise and transfer safely patients with brain injury
- **NA_IS_08** *(A,M)* — Shows sensitivity in giving support to patients and relatives during end of life care
- **NA_IS_09** *(A,M)* — Demonstrates ability to communicate well with the surgical team including ensuring the exchange of relevant information
- **NA_IS_10** *(A,C)* — Demonstrates the ability to select and use appropriate invasive monitoring when indicated in patients undergoing neurosurgical procedures
- **NA_IS_11** *(A,C)* — Demonstrates the ability to recognise and manage diabetes insipidus/SIADH
- **NA_IS_12** *(A,C)* — Demonstrates the ability to manipulate blood pressure as appropriate for the clinical situation
- **NA_IS_13** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates the ability to manage emergence from anaesthesia in a smooth and controlled way
- **NA_IS_14** *(A,C,D)* — Demonstrates the ability to manage neurosurgical patient in the immediate postoperative period

---

## Cardiothoracic Anaesthesia

### Skills — Cardiac

- **CT_IS_01** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates the ability to assess and give a risk assessment for patients presenting for cardiac surgery including those with valvular and ischaemic heart disease
- **CT_IS_02** *(A,C,D,M)* — Demonstrates the ability to assess patients with intra-thoracic aortic pathology such as aneurysm, dissection and coarctation, and give an informed judgement on the risks and benefits of anaesthesia and surgery
- **CT_IS_03** *(A,C,D,M)* — Demonstrates safe delivery of perioperative anaesthesia for a patient undergoing elective coronary bypass including management of a patient during cardiopulmonary bypass, a patient having cardiac surgery off bypass, and coagulation management
- **CT_IS_04** *(A,D)* — Forms postoperative care plans appropriate to the surgery and the patient's condition including postoperative analgesia and respiratory support
- **CT_IS_05** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates correct use of invasive and non-invasive monitoring in patients with cardiac or respiratory disease including non-invasive cardiac output monitoring devices utilising a variety of technologies, such as LIDCO, PICCO and ODM
- **CT_IS_07** *(A,C,D)* — Demonstrates the ability to provide anaesthesia for procedures in cardiac intensive care including re-sternotomy, re-intubation, tracheostomy and cardioversion
- **CT_IS_08** *(A,C,D)* — Performs anaesthesia for patients having cardiological electrophysiological procedures, including pacemaker insertion

### Skills — Thoracic

- **CT_IS_09** *(A,C,D)* — Demonstrates ability to assess and recommend treatments to optimise a patient about to undergo thoracic surgery
- **CT_IS_10** *(A,C,D,M)* — Demonstrates safe delivery of perioperative anaesthetic care to patients for minor thoracic procedures, in particular bronchoscopy, including the safe use of the Sanders injector
- **CT_IS_11** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates correct selection of appropriate airway management for the intended procedure and the ability to correctly insert single or double lumen endobronchial tubes and bronchial blockers
- **CT_IS_12** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates correct use of clinical and endoscopic methods to confirm correct tube placement
- **CT_IS_13** *(A,C,D)* — Demonstrates safe delivery of perioperative anaesthetic care for major thoracic procedures, including correct airway and ventilatory management, positioning and patient protection
- **CT_IS_14** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates the ability to manage a patient undergoing one lung ventilation
- **CT_IS_15** *(A,C,D,M)* — Demonstrates the ability to formulate correct post-operative care plans, taking into account the patient's condition and the surgical procedure, including an assessment of the need for management in intensive care or high dependency

## Airway Management

### Skills

- **AM_IS_01** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates elective fibreoptic intubation under anaesthesia with or without LMAs or other airway adjuncts
- **AM_IS_02** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates effective teaching of basic airway manoeuvres, direct laryngoscopy and endotracheal intubation to novice students (e.g. nurses, CT1 anaesthetic trainees, paramedics, medical students)
- **AM_IS_03** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates the use of supraglottic airways for IPPV
- **AM_IS_04** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates double lumen endobronchial tube placement and lung isolation, including the use of bronchial blockers and the use of clinical/endoscopic methods to confirm correct position

## General, Urological and Gynaecological Surgery

### Knowledge

- **GU_IK_12** *(A,C)* — Discusses the importance of the timing of non-elective surgery and the effect that this may have on the delivery of 'emergency surgery'

### Skills

- **GU_IS_01** *(A,C,D)* — Demonstrates the ability to recognise when it is/is not necessary to order complex preoperative assessment tests such as cardiopulmonary exercise testing and echocardiography prior to anaesthesia/surgery
- **GU_IS_02** *(L,M)* — Demonstrates the ability to manage safely and effectively the peri-operative care of patients requiring elective and/or emergency resection of the lower bowel or similar complexity urological and/or gynaecological case (open or laparoscopic), with distant supervision
- **GU_IS_03** *(A,C,D)* — Demonstrates the ability to manage the effects of sudden major blood loss effectively
- **GU_IS_04** *(L,M)* — Demonstrates the ability to work with all members of the theatre and surgical teams to manage an operating list with a mixture of ASA 1-3 cases effectively, along with the ability to provide safe peri-operative anaesthetic care for the patients
- **GU_IS_05** *(A,C,D,L,M)* — Shows the ability to lead (where appropriate) the theatre team in the perioperative management of surgical patients requiring out of hours surgery, including understanding of when to seek help appropriately
- **GU_IS_06** *(A,C,D,M)* — Demonstrates the ability to present a balanced judgement to the patient and their relatives of the perceived risks and complications of anaesthesia and surgery

## Head, Neck, Maxillo-facial and Dental Surgery (ENT)

### Skills

- **EN_IS_01** *(A,C)* — Develops and modifies the skills of pre-operative assessment and optimisation, and the administration of anaesthesia to include a focus on the special difficulties presented by these surgical sub-specialties
- **EN_IS_02** *(A,C,D)* — Demonstrates how to interpret CT and MRI scans of the head and neck
- **EN_IS_03** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates correct use of a variety of advanced airway devices
- **EN_IS_04** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates use of hypotensive techniques where indicated
- **EN_IS_05** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates safe anaesthesia/sedation for outpatient dental surgery
- **EN_IS_06** *(A,C,D,M)* — Demonstrates the safe perioperative anaesthetic management of more complex head, neck and maxillo-facial procedures including laser surgery, bronchoscopy, surgery on the middle ear, thyroid surgery, and maxillary and mandibular osteotomies
- **EN_IS_07** *(A,C,D,M)* — Demonstrates the safe perioperative anaesthetic management of head, neck, maxillo-facial and dental emergencies including bleeding tonsil, obstructed upper airway, obstructed lower airway, and mandibular and maxillary fractures
- **EN_IS_08** *(A,C,M)* — Demonstrates the ability to work with all members of the theatre and surgical teams to manage an operating list with a mixture of ASA 1-3 non-complex minor/intermediate cases effectively
- **EN_IS_09** *(A,C,M)* — Shows the ability to lead (where appropriate) the theatre team in the perioperative management of patients requiring out of hours minor/intermediate ENT, maxillo-facial and dental surgery, including understanding of when to seek help appropriately
- **EN_IS_10** *(A,C,D,M)* — Demonstrates the specific measures needed to provide appropriate analgesia and other postoperative care including oxygen therapy, airway monitoring, fluids and anti-emetics in patients following major head, neck, maxillo-facial and dental surgery

## Regional Anaesthesia

### Skills

- **RA_IS_01** *(A,C,D)* — Demonstrates safe perioperative management of patients receiving regional techniques including liaison with theatre staff, surgeons, recovery staff, acute pain teams and ward staff
- **RA_IS_02** *(A,C,D)* — Is able to perform central nerve blocks including caudal and thoracic epidural and CSE
- **RA_IS_03** *(A,C,D)* — Is able to perform major nerve blocks including upper limb blocks (minimum of one) and lower limb blocks (minimum of one)

## Sedation

### Knowledge

- **CK_IK_07** *(A,C,D)* — Discusses the use of sedation in the high-risk patient and the advantages/disadvantages of using general anaesthesia as opposed to sedation to cover necessary investigations/procedures in such patients

### Skills

- **CS_IS_01** *(A,C,D)* — Demonstrates the ability to select sedation techniques appropriate to management in patients of all ages (above 5 years to the elderly)
- **CS_IS_02** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates the ability to administer and monitor sedation techniques to all patients for appropriate clinical procedures, safely and effectively
- **CS_IS_03** *(A,D)* — Demonstrates the ability to consider: that no one technique is suitable for all patients and the most appropriate technique is based on minimum intervention; and that techniques using multiple drugs/anaesthetic drugs should only be considered where there is clear clinical justification having excluded simpler techniques
