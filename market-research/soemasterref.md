## Domain: Neuroanaesthesia

### Topic 1: Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)
*   **Common Scenarios:** 
    *   Emergency craniotomy for extradural/subdural haematoma.
    *   Transfer of the intubated head-injured patient.
    *   Poly-trauma patient with low GCS.
*   **Science Base (Anatomy/Physiology/Pharm):**
    *   [NA_IK_01] Anatomy of skull/CNS (Monro-Kellie doctrine).
    *   [NA_IK_02] CBF physiology, autoregulation, CO2 reactivity.
    *   [NA_IK_06] Pharmacology of drugs acting on CNS (propofol/volatiles effects on CMRO2/CBF).
*   **Clinical Management:**
    *   [NA_IK_03] Techniques for decreasing ICP.
    *   [NA_IK_20] Neurocritical care management (MAP targets, fluids, ventilation).
    *   [NA_IK_22] Safe transfer requirements.
*   **Stage 2 Capabilities (Behaviour/Safety):**
    *   [Team-A] Leadership in trauma calls.
    *   [Safety-B] Factors influencing reliable care (checklist use in transfer).
    *   [Safety-I] Medication safety (mannitol/hypertonic saline confusion).
*   **Critical Fails (Red Flags):**
    *   Failure to clear C-spine or assume injury in poly-trauma.
    *   Allowing hypotension (SBP < 100) or hypoxia during transfer.
    *   Hyperventilating to PaCO2 < 4.0 kPa without indication (causing ischaemia).
*   **Differentiation (Merit-Level):**
    *   Discussion of BTF (Brain Trauma Foundation) guidelines nuances.
    *   Debate on decompressive craniectomy evidence (RESCUE-ICP).
*   **Key Guidelines:**
    *   AAGBI: Safe Transfer of the Brain Injured Patient.
    *   NICE: Head Injury: Assessment and Early Management.

### Topic 2: Intracranial Haemorrhage & Vascular
*   **Common Scenarios:**
    *   Subarachnoid Haemorrhage (SAH) coiling/clipping.
    *   Posterior fossa surgery.
*   **Science Base:**
    *   [NA_IK_02] CSF circulation and hydrocephalus.
    *   [NA_IK_06] Vasospasm physiology and nimodipine.
*   **Clinical Management:**
    *   [NA_IK_10] Neuroradiology anaesthesia (coiling).
    *   [NA_IK_09] TIVA vs Volatile debate in neurosurgery.
*   **Stage 2 Capabilities:**
    *   [Prof-F] Breaking bad news (poor prognosis to relatives).
    *   [Team-D] MDT working with neurosurgeons/radiologists.
*   **Critical Fails (Red Flags):**
    *   Failure to control blood pressure pre-coiling (re-bleed risk).
    *   Failure to recognise hydrocephalus signs (bradycardia/hypertension).
*   **Differentiation (Merit-Level):**
    *   Discussion of 'Triple H' therapy vs current Euvolaemia guidelines.
    *   Management of intra-operative aneurysm rupture.
*   **Key Guidelines:**
    *   SNACC: Consensus Statement on Anaesthetic Management of patients with SAH.

## Domain: Cardiothoracic

### Topic 1: Ischaemic Heart Disease & CABG
*   **Common Scenarios:**
    *   Elective CABG pre-op assessment.
    *   Off-pump surgery complications.
    *   Emergency return to theatre for bleeding.
*   **Science Base:**
    *   [CT_IK_01] Cardiac anatomy and coronary blood flow.
    *   [CT_IK_16] Inotrope and vasodilator pharmacology.
*   **Clinical Management:**
    *   [CT_IK_02] CPB principles and cardioplegia.
    *   [CT_IK_09] Cardiac tamponade recognition.
    *   [CT_IK_12] Coagulation management (heparin/protamine/ACT).
*   **Stage 2 Capabilities:**
    *   [Periop-C] Shared decision making (high risk consent).
    *   [Safety-F] Proactive safety (WHO checklist in complex environments).
*   **Critical Fails (Red Flags):**
    *   Failure to recognise/treat tamponade physiology quickly.
    *   Inadequate heparinisation before CPB.
    *   Missing ischaemic changes on ECG during induction.
*   **Differentiation (Merit-Level):**
    *   Knowledge of specific coronary perfusion pressures/autoregulation limits.
    *   Evidence base for Off-Pump vs On-Pump outcomes (ROOBY/CORONARY trials).
*   **Key Guidelines:**
    *   ESC/EACTS Guidelines on myocardial revascularization.
    *   ACTA/SCATA Guidelines on anti-coagulation.

### Topic 2: Thoracic Surgery (Video-Assisted)

    *   Oesophagectomy.
*   **Science Base:**

*   **Stage 2 Capabilities:**

*   **Critical Fails (Red Flags):**

*   **Differentiation (Merit-Level):**
    *   Protective lung ventilation strategies in thoracic surgery.
    *   Enhanced recovery after thoracic surgery (ERAS).
*   **Key Guidelines:**
    *   BTS Guidelines on fitness for radical surgery.

## Domain: Airway Management

### Topic 1: The Anticipated Difficult Airway
*   **Common Scenarios:**
    *   Awake Fibreoptic Intubation (AFOI) for tumour/anatomy.
    *   Strategy for the morbidly obese patient.
*   **Science Base:**
    *   [AM_IK_02] Interpretation of flow-volume loops/CT neck.
    *   [AM_IK_12] Physics of flow (Bernoulli/Venturi) in jet ventilation.
*   **Clinical Management:**
    *   [AM_IK_01] Risks of AFOI and consent.
    *   [AM_IK_10] Supraglottic airway backup plans.
*   **Stage 2 Capabilities:**
    *   [Prof-C] Reflection on personal limits (when to call for help).
    *   [Safeguard-D] Consent in anxious/pre-medicated patients.
*   **Critical Fails (Red Flags):**
    *   Inducing anaesthesia without a secured airway plan in severe obstruction.
    *   Over-sedation during AFOI leading to apnea.
    *   Muscle relaxant administration before confirming ventilation ability (in uncertain airways).
*   **Differentiation (Merit-Level):**
    *   Role of High Flow Nasal Oxygen (HFNO/THRIVE) in difficult airway management.
    *   Dexmedetomidine vs Remifentanil for AFOI.
*   **Key Guidelines:**
    *   DAS Guidelines 2015 (Unanticipated Difficult Intubation).
    *   DAS Guidelines for Awake Tracheal Intubation.

### Topic 2: The Unanticipated Difficult Airway (CICO)
*   **Common Scenarios:**
    *   Can't Intubate Can't Oxygenate in obstetrics/routine lists.
    *   Displaced tracheostomy.
*   **Science Base:**
    *   [AM_IK_06] Physiology of apnoeic oxygenation.
*   **Clinical Management:**
    *   [AM_IK_13] Jet ventilation principles.
    *   [AM_IK_05] Front of Neck Access (FONA) anatomy/technique.
*   **Stage 2 Capabilities:**
    *   [Team-E] Managing complex dynamic situations (Crisis Resource Management).
*   **Critical Fails (Red Flags):**
    *   Fixation error (repeated attempts) leading to hypoxia/trauma.
    *   Failure to declare CICO and proceed to FONA.
    *   Failure to call for help early.
*   **Differentiation (Merit-Level):**
    *   Scalpel-bougie-tube vs Cannula technique debates (NAP4 findings).
    *   Human factors in airway crisis management (Vortex approach).
*   **Key Guidelines:**
    *   DAS Guidelines 2015.
    *   NAP4 Report findings (Royal College of Anaesthetists).

## Domain: Obstetric Anaesthesia

### Topic 1: Major Obstetric Haemorrhage
*   **Common Scenarios:**
    *   Placenta Previa/Accreta.
    *   PPH post-delivery.
*   **Science Base:**
    *   [OB_Gen] Physiology of pregnancy (haematological changes).
    *   [OB_Pharm] Uterotonics pharmacology (Oxytocin/Ergometrine/Carboprost).
*   **Clinical Management:**
    *   [GU_IK_07] Massive transfusion protocols.
    *   [Gen-C] Management of major haemorrhage.
*   **Stage 2 Capabilities:**
    *   [Safe-G] Matching resources to risk (Cell salvage, interventional radiology).
    *   [Team-A] Leading the obstetric emergency team.
*   **Critical Fails (Red Flags):**
    *   Failure to activate Major Haemorrhage Protocol early.
    *   Ignoring coagulopathy (DIC) while chasing blood pressure.
    *   Giving Ergometrine in severe Pre-Eclampsia.
*   **Differentiation (Merit-Level):**
    *   Cell Saver use in obstetrics (NICE/SALVO trial).
    *   Role of Tranexamic Acid (WOMAN trial).
    *   ROTEM/TEG guided Point-of-Care Testing (POCT).
*   **Key Guidelines:**
    *   RCOG Green-top Guideline No. 52: Prevention and Management of Postpartum Haemorrhage.
    *   OAA/AAGBI Guidelines for Obstetric Anaesthesia Services.

### Topic 2: Pre-Eclampsia & Hypertension
*   **Common Scenarios:**
    *   Emergency CS for fetal distress in PET.
    *   Eclamptic seizure management.
*   **Science Base:**
    *   [OB_Gen] Pathophysiology of endothelial dysfunction.
    *   [NA_IK_06] Magnesium pharmacology.
*   **Clinical Management:**
    *   [OB_Gen] Fluid balance in PET.
    *   [Gen-A] Neuraxial blockade in coagulopathy risks.
*   **Stage 2 Capabilities:**
    *   [Prof-A] Advising junior colleagues (midwives) on escalation.
*   **Critical Fails (Red Flags):**
    *   Fluid overload leading to pulmonary oedema (commonest cause of maternal death).
    *   Failure to control severe hypertension (BP > 160/110) leading to ICH.
    *   Spinal anaesthesia in severe thrombocytopenia (< 75).
*   **Differentiation (Merit-Level):**
    *   Pre-eclampsia toxicology/magnesium therapeutic levels.
    *   Invasive monitoring in severe PET (CVP vs Echo).
*   **Key Guidelines:**
    *   NICE Guideline NG133: Hypertension in pregnancy.
    *   MBRRACE-UK Reports (Maternal Mortality insights).

## Domain: Paediatric Anaesthesia

### Topic 1: The Sick Child (Sepsis/Emergency)
*   **Common Scenarios:**
    *   Meningococcal septicaemia.
    *   Acute epiglottitis/croup.
    *   Intussusception.
*   **Science Base:**
    *   [RC_IK_09] Differences in cardiac arrest aetiology (Adult vs Child).
    *   [Paed_Phy] Paediatric airway anatomy & physiology.
*   **Clinical Management:**
    *   [RC_IK_12] Intubation choice (cuffed/uncuffed).
    *   [RC_IK_14] Safe transfer to PICU.
*   **Stage 2 Capabilities:**
    *   [Safeguard-A] Child protection concerns (non-accidental injury).
    *   [Prof-E] Emotional regulation in paediatric emergencies.
*   **Critical Fails (Red Flags):**
    *   Using adult drug doses or miscalculating by factor of 10.
    *   Using inappropriate fluids (e.g., hypotonic solutions) causing hyponatraemia.
    *   Ignoring bradycardia (pre-arrest sign in children).
    *   Upsetting a child with epiglottitis/stridor before induction.
*   **Differentiation (Merit-Level):**
    *   Sepsis 6 for Paediatrics differences.
    *   IO access indications and evidence.
*   **Key Guidelines:**
    *   APLS (Advanced Paediatric Life Support) algorithms.
    *   APA (Association of Paediatric Anaesthetists) Guidelines.

### Topic 2: Routine Paediatrics & Day Case
*   **Common Scenarios:**
    *   Tonsillectomy (and bleeding tonsil).
    *   Squint surgery (POV risks).
*   **Science Base:**
    *   [DS_IK_01] Criteria for day case surgery in children.
*   **Clinical Management:**
    *   [EN_IK_11] Management of bleeding tonsil.
*   **Stage 2 Capabilities:**
    *   [Safeguard-B] Communication with parents/consent.
*   **Critical Fails (Red Flags):**
    *   Discharging a child who has had apnoeas post-op.
    *   Anaesthetising a child with a recent URTI without risk assessment (laryngospasm risk).
    *   Inadequate volume resuscitation in bleeding tonsil before induction.
*   **Differentiation (Merit-Level):**
    *   Risk stratification for URTI (COLD criteria).
    *   Pharmacokinetics of paracetamol/NSAIDs in neonates vs children.
*   **Key Guidelines:**
    *   APA Guidelines on the Management of the Child with an URTI.

## Domain: General & Emergency

### Topic 1: The Acute Abdomen & Sepsis
*   **Common Scenarios:**
    *   Emergency Laparotomy (perf DU/bowel obstruction).
    *   Ruptured AAA.
*   **Science Base:**
    *   [GU_IK_10] Sepsis pathophysiology and cardiac output monitoring.
*   **Clinical Management:**
    *   [GU_IK_09] Enhanced recovery principles (applied to emergency).
    *   [GU_IK_11] Risk stratification (P-POSSUM/NELA).
*   **Stage 2 Capabilities:**
    *   [Periop-N] End of life care (futility of surgery).
    *   [Periop-B] Liaison with ICU/Surgeons for bed planning.
*   **Critical Fails (Red Flags):**
    *   Inadequate fluid resuscitation prior to induction (cardiovascular collapse).
    *   Failure to recognise septic shock ("cold sepsis").
    *   Proceeding without NCEPOD classification/seniority discussion.
*   **Differentiation (Merit-Level):**
    *   Vasopressor choice in sepsis (Noradrenaline vs Vasopressin).
    *   NELA (National Emergency Laparotomy Audit) standards.
*   **Key Guidelines:**
    *   Surviving Sepsis Campaign Guidelines.
    *   NELA Reports.

### Topic 2: Trauma & Orthopaedics
*   **Common Scenarios:**
    *   NOF Fracture in the elderly comorbid patient.
    *   Cement Implantation Syndrome.
    *   Fat Embolism.
*   **Science Base:**
    *   [OR_IK_04] Blood conservation.
*   **Clinical Management:**
    *   [OR_IK_01] Hip fracture management guidance (AAGBI 'Blue Book').
    *   [Gen-G] Positioning injuries.
*   **Stage 2 Capabilities:**
    *   [Periop-Q] Frailty and cognitive impairment adjustments.
    *   [Legal-D] Mental Capacity Act (dementia patients).
*   **Critical Fails (Red Flags):**
    *   Spinal anaesthesia in severe aortic stenosis (unmanaged).
    *   Failure to stop anticoagulants appropriately for neuraxial block.
    *   BCIS (Cement Syndrome) unpreparedness (Not informing team, no Oxygen).
*   **Differentiation (Merit-Level):**
    *   Regional anaesthesia additives (Dexamethasone, Dexmedetomidine) evidence.
    *   Management of the anticoagulated trauma patient (DOAC reversal).
*   **Key Guidelines:**
    *   AAGBI 'Blue Book': Management of Proximal Femoral Fractures.
    *   BOAST Guidelines (British Orthopaedic Association).
